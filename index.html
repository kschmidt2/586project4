On the night of Friday, November 13, 2015, terrorists attacked several popular locations throughout Paris, including a soccer stadium hosting a France-Germany friendly, a concert hall hosting a metal concert and several restaurants, killing 129 people and wounding hundreds of others. Shortly after news of the attacks spread throughout the world, the Islamic State, known as ISIS or ISIL, claimed responsibility for the deadliest attacks on Paris since World War II. Stade de France

9:20 p.m., 9:30 p.m., 9:53 p.m.

After being turned away by stadium security, a suicide bomber detonated an explosives belt outside of Gate D, killing himself and one victim. The blast could be heard inside the stadium, where France and Germany were playing an exhibition soccer match. President Francois Hollande, who was inside the stadium, made the quick decision to not alert the players and fans. The match continued and no one was allowed to leave the building. It wound up being the right call as ten minutes later, another suicide bomber struck outside of Gate H, killing only himself. Twenty-three minutes after the second bomb, another suicide bomber struck not far from the stadium, also killing only himself. The total death toll at the Stade de France, which holds over 81,000 fans, was only one. Had the stadium been evacuated, the death toll would have been much higher.

Le Carillon and Le Petit Cambodge

9:25 p.m.

Five minutes after the attacks began at Stade de France, gunmen in a black vehicle shot at diners sitting at Le Carillon and the nearby restaurant Le Petit Cambodge with assault rifles. The attack on the two restaurants killed 15 and left 10 more seriously wounded.

Cafe Bonne Bière

9:32 p.m.

Five diners were killed and eight more critically wounded when gunmen in a black vehicle opened fire at Cafe Bonne Bière.

La Belle Equipe

9:36 p.m.

Gunmen in a black vehicle fired onto the terrace of La Belle Equipe, killing 19 people and critically wounding nine others.

Comptoir Voltaire

9:40 p.m.

A suicide bomber detonated an explosive device at the Comptoir Voltaire restaurant, seriously wounding one person.

Bataclan

9:40 p.m.

Three men attacked the Bataclan, a concert venue that holds 1,500 people, during a sold-out performance of the American band Eagles of Death Metal. They ran into the club's two-story ballroom throwing grenades and shooting the crowd throughout the hall. The band and some concertgoers escaped, but after the initial gunfire the gunmen began rounding up the remaining survivors and held them hostage as police officers amassed outside. Over two hours later, the police raided the concert hall. Two attackers exploded their suicide vests while the third attacker's was activated by police gunfire. The total death toll at the Bataclan was 89.
